Replace old galvanized/poly-B with PEX-B

Polybutylene is a gray plastic pipe material widely used in homes built between 1978-1995 because it was inexpensive and easy to install. It was later found to degrade when exposed to chlorine in treated tap water. Pipe failure releases water inside walls, damaging drywall and insulation. Water spreads to flooring and lower levels, potentially leading to mold growth and structural damage. This system is a primary target during inspections and often excludes properties from standard insurance water damage coverage.
